Video Recordings of the Experiments in 'Air-Surface Transition of a Novel Leg-Assisted Multirotor UAV: Design and Control'

Takeoff: SLQ is set to hover at initial altitude waiting for the take-off order. Once the order is received, rotors generate lift force to reach desired velocity(1$m/s$) while transiting to the air.

Landing: Our SLQ can land at -1.8$m/s$ under 1$s$ and reach the max acceleration 20$m/s^2$.

Hopping: In fixed point hopping experiment, we showcase the position keeping capability during continuous hopping.

Wall Contact: In order to exhibit the contact tolerance performance of our proposed approach, SLQ was set to fly towards a vertical wall at desired velocity(1.8$m/s$).
